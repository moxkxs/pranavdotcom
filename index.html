I'm Pranav, a trader interested in systematic strategies and market structure

Information geometry provides a mathematical framework for understanding probability distributions as curved manifolds, w here the "distance" between two beliefs can be measured rigorously. This perspective transforms statistical inference from a problem of parameter estimation into one of navigation through geometric space, where likelihood functions become surfaces to traverse. The Fisher information metric quantifies how much curvature exists in parameter space—high curvature means small changes in parameters lead to large changes in the distribution, making inference more difficult.

What's remarkable is that many seemingly disparate statistical procedures—maximum likelihood, Bayesian updating, and variational inference—can be understood as different paths through the same underlying geometric structure. This unification suggests that the topology of belief space itself constrains what's learnable, independent of any specific algorithm. In trading contexts, this framework offers insights into how information propagates through price distributions and why certain regimes are harder to learn than others.
