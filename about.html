About

A bit more about me and what I'm up to

Background

I'm a trader focused on systematic strategies, derivatives pricing, and market microstructure. Most of my time is spent building models, analyzing data, and thinking about how to construct robust trading systems that work across different market regimes.

My approach combines quantitative analysis with a deep interest in how markets actually function at the microstructure level. I'm less interested in prediction and more interested in understanding the mechanics of price formation, liquidity provision, and risk transfer.

What I'm Working On

Currently exploring opportunities in options market making and volatility trading. Building out infrastructure for backtesting, execution, and risk management. Spending a lot of time thinking about position sizing, portfolio construction, and how to think about tail risk.

I'm particularly interested in the intersection of information theory and markets—how information propagates through prices, what makes certain regimes learnable vs. unlearnable, and how to build systems that adapt to changing market dynamics.

Interests

Outside of trading: mathematics, probability theory, information geometry, Bayesian inference, complex systems, and computational statistics. I read a lot about game theory, mechanism design, and how to think about uncertainty and decision-making under ambiguity.

I'm also interested in the history and philosophy of markets—how they evolved, why they work (when they do), and how to think about their role in society beyond just being mechanisms for price discovery.

Get in Touch

Always happy to chat about markets, trading, or any of the topics above. Best way to reach me is via email or Twitter. If you're working on something interesting in quantitative finance or market microstructure, I'd love to hear about it.
